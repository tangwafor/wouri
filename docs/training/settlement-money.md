# Settlement and money

**Wouri** · Getting paid, and proving the money came home. No em-dashes.

Who: finance, export manager. Time: 10 minutes.

## The one rule: settled means repatriated

In Wouri a consignment is not settled when the buyer pays. It is settled when the
proceeds are **repatriated and domiciled** within the BEAC window. This matches the
CEMAC rule and it is the difference that protects you.

## Open a settlement

On a consignment detail page, the **Settlement** panel:

1. Choose the instrument: letter of credit, documentary collection, documents
   against payment or acceptance, open account, or advance.
2. Enter the amount and currency.
3. Enter the **export date**. This starts the repatriation clock.
4. Press **Open settlement**.

## The state machine

A settlement moves in order, and only in order:

**draft → presented → accepted → paid → repatriated**

Press the button for the next step. Wouri will not let you skip. You cannot mark
paid before accepted, and you cannot repatriate before paid.

## Discrepancies block the money

If there is an open discrepancy on the instrument (a document mismatch, a missing
paper), Wouri **blocks acceptance and payment** until it is resolved. This is how a
bad presentation is caught before it costs you.

## The BEAC clock

Once the export date is set, the panel shows the **repatriation clock**:

- Green: comfortable.
- Amber: 15 days or fewer remaining.
- Red: **overdue**. The proceeds have not come home within the window.

The same clock feeds your dashboard and the readiness board, so an approaching or
missed repatriation is never a surprise.

## Financing

Wouri records financing (warehouse receipts, a cash timeline) so a consignment can
back credit. Wouri records; it never lends.

## Tips

- Set the export date accurately; the clock depends on it.
- Watch the dashboard **Repatriation** card each morning.
