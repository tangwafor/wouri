# Wouri: Investor Pitch

The trust and credit layer for African commodity export. Market claims cited to 2024-2026 sources; refresh fast-moving figures (EUDR dates, penalties, quotas, export tonnage) at pitch time. No em-dashes.

## Narrative (why now, why this, why us)
On 30 December 2026 the EU Deforestation Regulation starts applying to every medium and large operator, and on 30 June 2027 to every micro and small one, so that no cocoa bean or plank of wood enters the EU without a filed, geolocated Due Diligence Statement and its reference number. No reference number means the container is held at the EU border. For Cameroon, the fourth largest cocoa exporter and a Congo Basin timber source, this is an existential, dated deadline that forces every serious exporter to buy tooling inside the next 6 to 18 months. Everyone chasing that deadline is building the same thing, a polygon and a PDF. Wouri builds the missing thing: one file per consignment, from purchase order to settlement, that carries an EUDR rail and a CITES rail at once, issues every document as a tamper-evident credential anyone can verify offline without trusting us, and quietly accumulates exactly the artifacts a pre-export lender needs. We are a registry of record, like a land registry or a classification society. The wedge is a deadline; the moat is that we turn compliance paperwork into a verifiable, bankable asset.

## Deck (11 slides)
1. **Problem.** African commodity export runs on a shoebox of disconnected PDFs; nothing ties to the physical lot; no third party can verify a claim without phoning the exporter. Cameroon has ~600,000 cocoa smallholders feeding its leading non-oil export, with no machine-readable paper trail. The same exporter often ships cocoa and protected-species timber under different regulators with zero shared record.
2. **Why now.** EUDR applies 30 Dec 2026 (large/medium), 30 Jun 2027 (micro/small); every EU-bound shipment needs a DDS reference number at customs or the goods are not released; penalty >= 4% of EU-wide turnover plus confiscation and bans, criminal liability up to 10 years under Directive 2024/1203; over 50,000 EU businesses bound plus non-EU suppliers.
3. **Solution: the consignment file.** One file, PO to settlement, producing every document, proving every claim, verifiable offline without trusting Wouri. A system of record, not a marketplace; never takes title, holds money, or lends.
4. **The wow: dual-rail lot + proof not trust.** One consignment carries an EUDR rail (polygons, version-pinned deforestation screen, DDS) and a CITES rail (appendix, annotation, permit, non-negative quota ledger). Each document is a W3C Verifiable Credential (standard since 15 May 2025) anchored to a public Merkle checkpoint, verified offline. No competitor serves both rails on one lot.
5. **Product.** Ingest offtake, polygons, warehouse receipt, grades, permits bound to one lot ID; screen EUDR + CITES (quota non-negative); issue offline-verifiable credentials; settle and hand off (the file is both export dossier and lender origination file).
6. **Market.** TAM: supply-chain traceability ~USD 6.7B in 2025, plus USD 350M+ incremental EUDR spend 2025-2027; the credit adjacency far larger at a USD 102.6B African trade-finance gap by 2027 (AfDB). SAM: African cocoa and timber exporters facing EUDR + CITES. SOM beachhead: Cameroon cocoa, 192k tonnes and USD 1.28B in 2024 (OEC/ONCC), concentrated among a few dozen licensed exporters, plus timber (XAF 140.4B forestry-product revenue 2024).
7. **Business model.** Core SaaS: per-consignment issuance fee + per-seat subscription, priced against a 4%-of-turnover downside. Verification take (later): banks, buyers, insurers pay to rely on and pull verified credentials. Credit origination (later, capital-light): a fee when a verified file originates pre-export finance. Never lends, never holds money. Comparable raises: Meridia EUR 5.2M Series A (incl. ICE); Koltiva Series A (AC Ventures).
8. **Moat and network effects.** The dual-rail lot; proof not trust (classification-society reliance moat); the effective-dated regulatory registry as a platform-owned common good (rules move constantly: EUDR delayed twice, banned species 31->76->91, CEMAC ban by 2028; one verified rule helps every tenant, no redeploy); a reputation flywheel from settled files.
9. **Competition (Wouri owns the intersection).** EUDR tools (Koltiva, Sourcemap, Meridia, TraceX) do polygons + DDS but not CITES quotas or verifiable credentials; timber tools (TIMFLOW, Double Helix) do chain-of-custody but not EUDR; trade-finance platforms do settlement but not compliance. Wouri does all five: polygons + DDS, CITES + non-negative quota, offline-verifiable credentials, effective-dated registry, credit-ready origination file.
10. **Go-to-market.** Beachhead: Cameroon cocoa (concentrated, one shared dated trigger); expand within the tenant (timber lots activate the CITES rail with no new sales motion); the diaspora angle (Netherlands takes the bulk of the beans); then CEMAC timber before 2028, then Ghana/Cote d'Ivoire cocoa, then coffee and rubber.
11. **Team, traction, ask.** Solo technical founder operating an AI build fleet shipping production multi-tenant, RLS-hardened, regulated SaaS across a dozen live apps. Traction plan: sign 1-3 cocoa exporters as design partners before the deadline, file real DDS numbers, issue the first bank-relied credentials, activate the timber rail, originate the first finance file. The ask: a seed round to harden the dual-rail engine and the credential issuer and convert design partners into paid consignment volume before the deadline forces the market to choose a vendor.

## Three lines an investor remembers
1. EUDR turned a shoebox of PDFs into a hard border check with a date on it, and Cameroon's exporters must buy tooling before 30 December 2026 or their cargo does not move.
2. Everyone else ships a polygon and a PDF; Wouri ships one file that carries EUDR and CITES at once and can be verified offline without trusting us, because value comes from third parties relying on the stamp.
3. We never lend and never hold money; we just hold the one file a lender needs, which is how a compliance cost becomes reputation collateral and a smallholder-sourcing exporter becomes bankable.
