Why now, why this, why us

On 30 December 2026 the EU Deforestation Regulation starts applying to every medium and large operator, and on 30 June 2027 to every micro and small one, so that no cocoa bean or plank of wood enters the EU without a filed, geolocated Due Diligence Statement and its reference number. No reference number means the container is held at the EU border. For Cameroon, the fourth largest cocoa exporter on earth and a Congo Basin timber source, this is an existential, dated deadline. Everyone chasing it is building the same thing, a polygon and a PDF. Wouri builds the missing thing: one file per consignment, from purchase order to settlement, that carries an EUDR rail and a CITES rail at once, issues every document as a tamper-evident credential anyone can verify offline without trusting us, and quietly accumulates exactly the artifacts a pre-export lender needs. We are a registry of record, like a land registry or a classification society. The wedge is a deadline; the moat is that we turn compliance paperwork into a verifiable, bankable asset.

The deck, eleven slides

Problem

  • African commodity export runs on a shoebox of disconnected PDFs; nothing ties to the physical lot and no third party can verify a claim without phoning the exporter.
  • Cameroon has roughly 600,000 cocoa smallholders feeding its leading non-oil export, with no machine-readable paper trail.
  • The same exporter often ships cocoa and protected-species timber, each under a different regulator, with zero shared system of record.

Why now

  • EUDR applies 30 Dec 2026 (large and medium), 30 Jun 2027 (micro and small).
  • Every EU-bound shipment needs a DDS reference number at customs, or the goods are not released.
  • Penalty at least 4% of EU-wide turnover, plus confiscation and market bans; criminal liability up to 10 years under Directive 2024/1203.
  • Over 50,000 EU businesses are already bound, plus their non-EU suppliers.

Solution: the consignment file

  • One file per consignment, purchase order to settlement, that produces every document, proves every claim, and is verifiable by anyone holding the paper, offline, without trusting Wouri.
  • A system of record, not a marketplace. Never takes title, holds money, or lends.

The wow: dual-rail lot + proof not trust

  • One consignment carries an EUDR rail (polygons, version-pinned deforestation screen, DDS) and a CITES rail (appendix, annotation, permit, a quota ledger that can never go negative).
  • Each document is a W3C Verifiable Credential (a standard since 15 May 2025) anchored to a public checkpoint, verified cryptographically and offline.
  • No competitor serves both rails on one lot.

Product

  • Ingest the offtake, polygons, warehouse receipt, grades and permits, all bound to one lot ID.
  • Screen the EUDR and CITES checks; the quota ledger is enforced non-negative.
  • Issue every artifact as an offline-verifiable credential.
  • Settle and hand off: the file is both the export dossier and the lender origination file.

Market

  • TAM: supply-chain traceability about $6.7B in 2025, with $350M+ incremental EUDR spend 2025 to 2027; the credit adjacency far larger at a $102.6B African trade-finance gap by 2027.
  • SAM: African cocoa and timber exporters facing EUDR plus CITES.
  • SOM beachhead: Cameroon cocoa, 192k tonnes and $1.28B in 2024, concentrated among a few dozen licensed exporters, plus timber.

Business model

  • Core SaaS: per-consignment issuance fee plus per-seat subscription, priced against a 4%-of-turnover downside.
  • Verification take (later): banks, buyers and insurers pay to rely on and pull verified credentials.
  • Credit origination (later, capital-light): a fee when a verified file originates pre-export finance. Never lends, never holds money.

Moat and network effects

  • The dual-rail lot no EUDR-only or timber-only tool offers.
  • Proof not trust: the classification-society economic moat, value from third-party reliance.
  • The effective-dated regulatory registry as a platform-owned common good; one verified rule helps every tenant, no redeploy.
  • A reputation flywheel: every settled file thickens the credit record.
